The Framework

The Mat Mindset
Method

Every session. Every match. Every setback. Run it through the same four-step system and nothing stays in your head rent-free. You observe it, own it, reframe it, and show up better next time.

01
Observe
What actually happened? No story, no blame — just the facts of the moment.
02
Interpret
What did it mean to you? Surface the belief that formed in that moment.
03
Reframe
How can you see it differently? Turn "I'm not good enough" into "that's a skill I can train."
04
Apply
One thing you'll do next time. Concrete. Actionable. On the mat.
"Pressure is opportunity.
Excellence is a habit.
Good people, good wrestling."
The 6-Week Backbone

Structure the
Mental Game

One theme per week — repeat the cycle through your season. Each week comes with journaling prompts built to expose what's actually going on between your ears.

1
Awareness
Understand how you think, prepare, react.
▶
"When do I feel most focused in practice?"
"What mental habits show up before matches?"
"Where does my attention drift under fatigue?"
2
Identity
Values, motivation, who you compete as.
▶
"Who am I as a wrestler when no one is watching?"
"What three values guide how I train?"
"Whose approval am I chasing — and is it mine?"
3
Confidence
Reframe pressure. Build preparation habits.
▶
"What evidence proves I belong on this mat?"
"How do I talk to myself in the third period?"
"What preparation ritual makes me feel ready?"
4
Control
Emotional regulation & energy management.
▶
"What triggers me to tighten up mid-match?"
"How do I reset my breath between periods?"
"What drains my energy the week of a comp?"
5
Resilience
Failure, setbacks, bounce-back routines.
▶
"What did my last loss actually teach me?"
"How fast do I bounce back after a bad rep?"
"What's my routine after a disappointing match?"
6
Purpose
Tie it all to your season goals.
▶
"Why am I doing this season?"
"What does success look like beyond wins?"
"What legacy do I want on this team?"
